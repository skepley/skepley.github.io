Bio

I did my Ph.D. in Mathematics at Florida Atlantic University co-advised by Jason Mireles James and Bill Kalies. I defended my dissertation in December 2017 and worked as a Hill Assistant Professor in the Mathematics department at Rutgers from 2018 until 2021. Currently I'm a post-doctoral researcher at Vrije Universiteit in Amsterdam. The picture above is quite old and no longer looks anything like me, as per tradition.

Research Interests

My research focuses on developing and applying computational methods to study nonlinear dynamical systems. I frequently work on novel algorithms based on functional analytic and topological methods which are made rigorous using computer assisted-proof. I am particularly interested in applications of these algorithms arising in the study of Gene Regulatory Networks and Celestial Mechanics.
